As per U.S. GAAP, the revenue growth rate is 33% and the net income growth is 40%. The strong performance is a culmination of strategic initiatives taken by the Company on several fronts, notably in the areas of relationship management, deepening of competencies and associate delight.
We added 138 customers in fiscal 2007, including seven Fortune 500 customers. The number of customers billing more than US$10m increased to 35 from 27 during the year, while the number of customers billing more than US$1m increased by 20% to 180. The revenue from consulting and enterprise business solutions grew 43% in the year, indicating our dominant position in this segment.
We continue to win the confidence of our prominent customers, resulting in opportunities of significant size and criticality. Our recent win of a US$200m deal from Applied Materials in Q4 based on managed services model is illustrative of this trend. This has been complemented by our successful execution capabilities resulting in higher growth of such accounts.
We continue to service our customers in areas of strategic importance such as delivering greater proportion of work from offshore. Significant progress was made in this area in fiscal 2007, leading to an increase in the offshore contribution to 49% of revenues compared to 45% in fiscal 2006. This was driven by a double-digit offshore volume growth in each of the quarters.
The net addition of associates during the year is around 11,000, taking the total organization strength to around 40,000 at the close of this year. A noteworthy feature of the year has been the reduction in associate attrition to 15.7% from 19.2% in fiscal 2006. Annualized quarterly attrition for Q4 was 13%.
We are gratified by the fact that our People Practices have won several recognitions in the global and regional arena in the past few months, which is an attestation of our renewed focus and commitment in this area. The latest feather in the cap is being accorded the number two position in the Hewitt Best Employers in India survey. We also featured amongst the top 12 in the Hewitt Best Employers in Asia survey, the only IT services company from India in the top 20 rankings.

We continue to see a strong demand in the marketplace and are well positioned to address that demand. Against this backdrop, we look forward to a revenue growth of 28% to 30% in fiscal 2008 as per U.S. GAAP. We expect to record a revenue of US$1.9b in fiscal 2008, at the higher end of our revenue guidance. The EPADS is expected to grow in the range of 27% to 29%. Corresponding Indian GAAP consolidated guidance after factoring in a rupee appreciation of 6% would translate to revenue growth rate of 20% to 22% and an EPS growth of 18% to 20%.

In Q4 our revenues grew 9.5% sequentially in U.S. dollar terms and over 7% in rupee terms, as rupee appreciated 1.5% during the quarter. Net profit for the quarter grew 17% sequentially on the back of the higher revenue growth.
Sequential volume growth of the parent company was 9.5%, with offshore volumes growing at 12%. Offshore billing rates were up by 0.7% and onsite rates increased by 0.6%. EBITDA margins for fiscal '06 are expected to be at the same level as fiscal '07 after considering salary increments, over 6% rupee appreciation and absorbing a charge of US$20m on account of RSUs given in fiscal '07.
Net manpower addition was 165 [sic - see documentation] in Q4 for the parent company and includes 600 ELTPs, that is freshers. We expect the gross manpower addition for fiscal '08 to be around 14,000 to 15,000.

For fiscal '08, Nipuna is expected to achieve revenue of US$61m, a 60% year-on-year growth, and is expected to do positive at the net profit level.
Turning to the U.S. GAAP, revenue for the quarter rose 9.5% sequentially to US$411.3m. And net income for the quarter was US$86.3m, a sequential growth of 21% after factoring in a stock compensation charge of US$5.9m.

For fiscal '07, we have flat operating margin compared to fiscal '06 and we expect that trend to continue in fiscal '08 also. Earning per ADS is expected to grow between 27% to 29%, marginally lower than the net income growth because of 2% plus dilution in the share count on account of exercise of options by our associates.
