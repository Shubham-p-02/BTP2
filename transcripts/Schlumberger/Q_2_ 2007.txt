ANDREW GOULD: So those wells as they come online, they bring on new production even though they may have been drilled two months ago. So as long as there is a spot market for a backlog in stimulation, there is gas production behind pipe where the wells have already been drilled.
GEOFF KIEBURTZ: Is your sense that that backlog has increased or decreased over the last--
ANDREW GOULD: No, we think it's decreasing at the moment.

ANDREW GOULD: We're certainly hearing talk of plans for a greater scope of our work going forward, yes.

ANDREW GOULD: No, I think we just feel a lot more comfortable about it.

ANDREW GOULD: Well, in as much as we're the only company who has all those services in-house, that is to say particularly seismic, P&M, wireline and well testing, obviously, that gives you more leverage to be efficient in your integration than perhaps people who have to do it from different companies because that's what traditionally the operators would have done.
